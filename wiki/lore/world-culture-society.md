# World Culture & Society

## Overview

The cultural and social fabric of the game world, based on the world lore bible template and historical [[feudal-system-md|research on medieval society]]. This page documents the planned cultural systems.

## Customs & Traditions

Based on the feudal research, the game world's customs likely include:
- **[[Homage]] ceremonies**: Ritual kneeling and oath-swearing between lord and [[vassal]]
- **[[Investiture]]**: Formal granting of a [[fief]] with symbolic objects (clod of earth, banner)
- **Court gatherings**: Lords hosting vassals for counsel, judgment, and social bonding
- **Feast and festival cycles**: Celebrations marking agricultural seasons, religious holidays, and court events

## Religion & Beliefs

Historical basis for in-game religious systems:
- Parallel institutional hierarchy (Pope → Archbishop → Bishop → Abbot → Parish Priest)
- Church as a major landowner (20-30% of productive land in some regions)
- Potential for church-crown tension (investiture conflicts)
- Canon law operating parallel to secular feudal law

## Economy & Trade

See [[medieval economy]] for detailed research. The game world's economy should reflect:
- Manorial self-sufficiency with surplus trade
- Weekly markets and annual fairs
- The tension between land-based and monetary wealth
- The rise of independent [[burgher]] towns outside the feudal system

## Law & Justice

Historical basis for in-game legal systems:
- **Decentralized justice**: Every lord exercised judicial authority over their tenants
- **High justice**: Power of life and death (reserved for king or highest lords)
- **Low justice**: Minor disputes and manorial offenses
- **Customary law**: Based on local tradition rather than written codes
- **Trial methods**: By ordeal (banned after 1215), by combat, by jury (emerging)

## Class Dynamics

The [[three estates]] model provides an ideological framework:
- Those who pray (clergy) — spiritual salvation
- Those who fight (nobility) — physical protection  
- Those who work (peasants) — material sustenance

In practice, the game may explore tensions between classes, including peasant uprisings, church-crown conflicts, and noble rivalries.
