# Kingdom Come: Deliverance

## Overview

- **Developer**: [[Warhorse Studios]]
- **Publisher**: Deep Silver
- **Release**: February 2018 (PC, PS4, Xbox One)
- **Genre**: Action RPG, Open World
- **Metacritic**: 76-78/100 (PC)
- **Sales**: Over 3 million copies by 2020; crowdfunded via Kickstarter ($1.1M raised in 2014)

## Key Feudal Mechanics

- Rigid medieval class system — player begins as a commoner and rises through social hierarchy
- Feudal lord dynamics — pledging service to Lord Radzig Kobyla
- Period-accurate economy — trade, blacksmithing, herbalism, and commerce grounded in 15th-century Bohemian feudalism
- Reputation system tied to social standing and noble patronage
- No magic or fantasy elements; strictly historical medieval simulation

## Design Significance

Sets the standard for historical authenticity in medieval games. Player begins at the bottom of the [[feudal hierarchy]] and must climb, providing a unique perspective on feudal power structures.

A sequel, [[Kingdom Come: Deliverance II]] (2025), expanded feudal politics with deeper noble intrigue and estate management.
