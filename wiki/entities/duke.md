# Duke

## Overview

A [[duke]] (Latin: *dux* — leader, military commander) ruled over a **duchy**, typically a large territory encompassing multiple counties. In the [[feudal hierarchy]], the duke ranked below the [[king]] and above the [[count]] and [[baron]].

## Role and Power

- Near-sovereign within their domain
- Maintained private armies
- Dispensed justice within the duchy
- Collected taxes
- Granted [[fief|fiefs]] to [[count|counts]] and [[baron|barons]]

## Historical Examples

**[[Duke William of Normandy]]** (later [[William the Conqueror]]) commanded more resources than the [[King of France]] to whom he owed nominal allegiance — highlighting the paradoxical nature of feudal hierarchies where a vassal could be more powerful than their lord.

## Relationship to Other Ranks

- **Granted fiefs by**: The [[king]] (or the [[Holy Roman Emperor]] in the German context)
- **Granted fiefs to**: [[count|count]]s, [[baron|barons]], and sometimes [[knight|knights]] directly
- **Military obligation**: Provide significant military support to the king; maintain their own standing forces

## Game Design Application

Dukes represent major power centers that can challenge the crown. They are natural candidates for:
- Major vassal characters with significant autonomy
- Potential rebel leaders or kingmakers
- Regional governors with their own courts and councils
- Complex political figures balancing loyalty to the king with self-interest
