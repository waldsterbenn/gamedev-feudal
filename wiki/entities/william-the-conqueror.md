# William the Conqueror (Duke William of Normandy)

## Overview

William, Duke of Normandy, invaded England in **1066** and became [[King]] of England after the Battle of Hastings. His conquest imposed [[feudalism]] wholesale on England, creating the most centralized feudal system in Europe.

## Key Achievements

- **Domesday Book (1086)**: Complete survey of English landholdings — 12,400+ settlements recorded, population estimated at 1.5-2 million
- **Crown land retention**: Kept ~20% of land under direct crown control — more than any continental ruler
- **Salisbury Oath (1086)**: Required all major landowners to swear direct allegiance to the king, overriding sub-vassal loyalties (a uniquely English feature)
- **Sheriff system**: Royal officials (shire reeves) enforced the king's writ at county level

## The Normandy Paradox

As Duke of Normandy, William was technically a vassal of the [[King of France]], yet he commanded far more resources and territory than his feudal lord — a paradox that fueled centuries of Anglo-French conflict.
