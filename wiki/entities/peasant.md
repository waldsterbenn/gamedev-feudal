# Peasants (Laboratores)

## Overview

Peasants were the foundation of the [[feudal hierarchy]], making up 60-90% of the population. Representing the "those who work" order in the [[three estates]] model, they provided material sustenance for all of medieval society.

## Categories

### Freemen
- Legally free but economically dependent
- Paid rent to the [[lord]] but could move, marry, and own property
- Often held land by lease or customary right

### Serfs (Villeins)
- Bound to the land — could not leave without permission
- Required to work the lord's demesne land (typically 2-3 days per week)
- Owed additional obligations: milling at the lord's mill, baking at the lord's oven, marriage fees, death duties
- Could not be bought/sold individually (unlike slaves) but transferred with the land

### Slaves
- Existed in early medieval period (particularly Anglo-Saxon England before Norman Conquest)
- Gradually absorbed into serfdom by the 12th century

## Economic Role

Peasants worked scattered strips in open fields for their own subsistence while laboring on the [[lord]]'s demesne as their feudal obligation. The [[manor]] system organized their lives around agricultural cycles, common land usage, and village community.

## Resistance and Agency

Despite their low status, peasants exercised agency through:
- **Peasant revolts**: Wat Tyler uprising (1381), Jacquerie (1358)
- **Post-Black Death leverage**: Labor shortage empowered demands for better conditions
- **Migration**: Freemen could move to towns to escape manorial obligations

## Game Design Application

In feudal games, peasants should not be faceless resource generators. They can be:
- NPCs with individual personalities, needs, and grievances
- A source of tension when obligations become excessive
- The foundation of economic viability — their satisfaction affects domain productivity
- Potential sources of rebellion when pushed too far
