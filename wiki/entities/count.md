# Count / Earl

## Overview

A [[count]] (Latin: *comes* — companion) or [[Earl:Earl]] (Anglo-Saxon, from Old Norse *jarl*) administered counties/shires as regional governors and military leaders in the [[feudal hierarchy]].

## Role

- Administered counties or shires
- Served as regional governors and military leaders
- Collected taxes and administered courts
- Kept a portion of fines and tolls as personal revenue
- Granted [[fief|fiefs]] to [[baron|barons]] and lesser nobles

## Etymology and Regional Variants

- **Count**: From Latin *comes* (companion of the [[king]])
- **Earl**: Anglo-Saxon equivalent (Old Norse *jarl*)
- Both ranks functioned similarly but reflected different cultural traditions

## Relationship to Other Ranks

- **Granted fiefs by**: [[King]] or [[duke]]
- **Granted fiefs to**: [[Baron]], [[knight|knights]], and lesser nobles
