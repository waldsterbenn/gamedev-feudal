# Lord

## Overview

A lord was any person who held land and had [[vassal|vassals]] owing them service. In the [[feudal hierarchy]], lords existed at multiple levels — from [[king]] down to [[baron]] — with each lord simultaneously being a vassal to someone above them (except the king) and a lord to those below.

## Roles and Responsibilities

### Owed to Vassals
- **Protection**: Military defense of vassal lands
- **Justice**: Fair adjudication of disputes in the lord's court
- **Maintenance**: Ensuring vassals could sustain their station

### Owed to Own Lord
- All standard vassal obligations: military service, counsel, financial aids

### Revenue Management
- Peasant labor on demesne
- Taxes, tolls, and justice fees
- Market and fair revenues
- Feudal aids from own vassals

## The Lord's Court

Every lord maintained a court that served as:
- Administrative center for managing the domain
- Judicial body for settling disputes
- Social gathering for reinforcing bonds with vassals
- Political arena for alliances and intrigue

## Game Design Application

The lord is the primary player role in a feudal game. The "lord fantasy" combines:
- Control (shaping a domain)
- Growth (watching the domain expand)
- Social connection (relationships with vassals)
- Competence (mastering complex systems)
- Identity ("I am a lord shaping the fate of a kingdom")
