# Medieval Economy

## Overview

The medieval economy evolved from a land-based, labor-obligation system toward an increasingly monetized economy. This transition played a key role in the eventual [[decline of feudalism]].

## Economic Phases

### Early Period (9th-11th centuries)
- **Limited coin circulation**: Much operated on labor obligations and in-kind payments
- **Silver penny (denier)**: The dominant coin
- **Self-sufficient manors**: The [[manor]] produced everything it needed internally
- **Surplus trade**: Weekly markets and annual fairs traded local surplus

### Transition Period (12th-13th centuries)
- **Monetization**: By the 13th century, money economy increased dramatically
- **Commutation**: Labor services converted to cash payments (rent)
- **Urban growth**: Towns developed outside the feudal land-tenure system
- **Merchant class**: New wealth based on trade and banking, not land

### Late Period (14th-15th centuries)
- **Labor scarcity**: After the Black Death, peasants demanded better wages
- **Statutes failed**: Attempts to freeze wages made feudal labor relations unenforceable
- **Free cities**: Some cities purchased charters and became effectively independent
- **Direct taxation**: Replaced feudal dues

## Revenue Sources for Nobles

### King's Revenue
- Crown lands
- Feudal aids (payments on special occasions)
- Reliefs (inheritance tax on fiefs)
- Scutage (payment in lieu of military service)
- Various taxes and tolls

### Lord's Revenue
- Peasant labor on demesne
- Taxes and tolls
- Milling and baking monopolies
- Justice fees from manorial courts

## The Rise of Towns

Towns presented a fundamental challenge to feudalism:
- **Burgers/Burghers**: Townspeople who were legally free and economically independent
- **Free cities**: Effectively independent of feudal obligations
- **Italian city-states**: Venice, Florence, Genoa governed by merchant oligarchies
- **New wealth**: Based on trade and banking rather than land

## Game Design Application

Economy systems should:
- Avoid the #1 failure mode: late-game economy saturation where gold becomes meaningless
- Scale with domain size: scarcity in early game, meaningful investment choices in late game
- Provide meaningful resource sinks (prestige projects, castle upgrades, court events)
- Support multiple income paths (taxes, trade, production)
- Use short, meaningful production chains rather than complex micromanagement
