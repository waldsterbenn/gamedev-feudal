# Market Analysis: Medieval Games

## Overview

Steam market analysis for medieval/feudal games based on survey of 18 titles, including pricing, wishlists, review sentiment, and market saturation assessment.

## Pricing Analysis

| Price Band | Examples | Reception |
|------------|----------|-----------|
| $9.99-$14.99 | Against the Storm ($17.99 launch), Medieval Dynasty ($19.99) | Accessible entry point, high volume |
| $14.99-$19.99 | Manor Lords ($39.99), Bannerlord ($49.99), KCD II ($59.99) | Premium range for deep experiences |
| $24.99+ | [[CK3]] ($49.99 base), Total War series | Requires exceptional reputation or AAA backing |

**Sweet spot**: $14.99-$19.99 for indie medieval games. Going above $25 requires exceptional quality or established franchise.

## Review Sentiment

- **80%+ positive** is the target threshold for healthy Steam presence
- **#1 review killer**: Early access abandonment without consistent updates
- Best-reviewed games share regular content updates, responsive developers, clear roadmaps
- Against the Storm achieved "Overwhelmingly Positive" (95%+) through exceptional EA management

## Effective Steam Tags

- "Medieval" + "City Builder" + "Management" + "Simulation" + "Colony Sim"
- "Grand Strategy" + "Medieval" + "RPG" + "Diplomacy"
- **Emerging niche tag**: "Kingdom Management" — growing in usage, not yet saturated

## Wishlist Strategy

- **Minimum threshold**: 10,000 wishlists for viable Steam launch
- **Target**: 25,000-50,000+ for strong algorithm push
- **Steam Next Fest is the #1 wishlist generator**: Manor Lords gained 1M+ wishlists through Next Fest appearances
- **Conversion rate**: 30-50% of wishlists convert to sales at launch
- **Timeline**: 4-6 months of pre-launch marketing standard for the genre

## Market Saturation

### Saturated Areas
- Generic medieval city builders
- Survival-craft medieval games (Valheim gold rush)
- Low-quality tactical RPGs

### White Space / Underserved
- **Feudal politics and relationship management**
- **Casual/educational feudal games**
- **Cooperative feudal gameplay**
- **Narrative-driven feudal experiences with ethical design**

## Success Case Studies

### Manor Lords (Solo Developer, $60M+ Week One)
- What worked: Incredible visual fidelity, deep but accessible city building, authentic medieval feel, aggressive community transparency
- Key lesson: Single developer with clear vision + exceptional quality in one core area > mediocre attempt at everything

### Against the Storm (Small Team, 500K+ Sales)
- What worked: Unique roguelite + colony builder hybrid, short replayable runs, constant EA updates
- Key lesson: Innovation in mechanical combination creates defensibility

### Medieval Dynasty (Render Cube, 1.5M+ During EA)
- What worked: Relaxing medieval life simulation, first-person immersion, dynasty/lineage hook
- Key lesson: The "medieval life" fantasy is underserved; players want to live in a medieval world

## Market Conclusion

The medieval game market is **moderately established but not saturated** in feudal social simulation and relationship-based gameplay. A casual, educational approach to feudalism has genuine market whitespace.
