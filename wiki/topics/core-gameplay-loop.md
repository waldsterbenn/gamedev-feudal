# Core Gameplay Loop

## Overview

The core gameplay loop describes the primary cycle of player actions, feedback, progression, and motivation. For the Feudal game, this loop is being designed based on both project templates and market research from comparable games.

## Template Structure (Current Draft)

The current design document outlines a four-phase loop:

1. **Player Action**: What the player does in the game (movement, decisions, interactions, management)
2. **Feedback**: What response the game provides (visual, audio, narrative, mechanical)
3. **Progression**: How the player advances (new content unlocked, abilities gained, territory expanded)
4. **Motivation**: What drives the player to continue the cycle (goals, story, rewards, challenge)

## Recommended Loop from Research

Based on analysis of successful feudal games, particularly the [[feudal game design]] insights from the market report, a more specific recommended loop is:

1. **Observe** — View your domain and its needs (harvest season, [[vassal]] tensions, trade opportunities)
2. **Decide** — Issue orders, mediate disputes, assign roles, respond to events
3. **Watch** — See consequences play out; vassals react; your [[manor|domain]] evolves
4. **Adapt** — New situations emerge; adjust strategy; prepare for succession

This loop avoids repetition through **emergent events** and **character-driven stories**, not through grinding resource collection.

## Key Considerations

- The loop must remain engaging across multiple play sessions
- Each cycle should present new information or choices
- [[Lord fantasy psychology]] suggests the loop should alternate between granular control and grand overview
- Avoid micromanagement death spirals
- Ensure the loop matters at both early and late game stages
