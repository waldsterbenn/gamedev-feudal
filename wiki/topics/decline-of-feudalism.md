# Decline of Feudalism

## Overview

Feudalism declined over several centuries (14th-15th centuries) due to multiple interconnected factors. No single cause destroyed it — rather, several forces combined to make the system untenable.

## The Black Death (1347-1351)

- **Death toll**: 30-50% of European population
- **Economic effect**: Massive labor shortage empowered peasants to demand better wages
- **Feudal response**: Statutes tried to freeze wages and bind peasants to the land (e.g., Statute of Labourers 1351 in England)
- **Result**: [[Peasant revolts]] everywhere; feudal labor relations became unenforceable

## Professional Armies

- **Standing armies**: [[King]]s began hiring professional soldiers instead of relying on feudal levies
- **Gunpowder weapons**: Cannons (14th c.) made [[castle]] walls obsolete; firearms reduced the importance of trained knights
- **Cost**: Professional armies required taxation — the state needed bureaucracy to collect and spend money

## Rise of Towns and Commerce

- **Urban centers**: Developed outside the feudal land-tenure system
- **Free cities**: Purchased charters and became effectively independent
- **Merchant class**: New wealth based on trade and banking, not land
- **Italian city-states**: Venice, Florence, Genoa governed by merchant oligarchies

## Centralization of Power

- **Nation-states**: [[King|Kings]] accumulated power at the expense of feudal [[vassal|vassals]]
- **Bureaucracy**: Professional administrators replaced feudal officials
- **Taxation**: Direct taxes (hearth tax, poll tax) replaced feudal dues
- **Monopoly on violence**: The emerging state claimed exclusive right to make war and dispense justice

## Timeline of Decline

| Period | Dates | Key Events |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| High Feudalism Crisis | 1200-1350 | Rise of towns, money economy; Magna Carta limits royal power |
| Black Death | 1347-1351 | 30-50% population killed; labor scarcity |
| Hundred Years' War | 1337-1453 | Professional armies prove superiority over feudal levies |
| Peasant Revolts | 1358-1381 | Jacquerie, Wat Tyler uprising — feudal labor unenforceable |
| Final Collapse | 1300s-1500 | Gunpowder, nation-states, rise of centralized monarchies |

## Game Design Application

The decline of feudalism provides natural story arcs and endgame scenarios:
- Transition periods create the most dramatic tension
- Players can either resist or embrace centralization
- Different paths to power emerge (military, economic, political)
- The end of feudalism is the beginning of something new
