# Recommended Mechanics Priority

## Overview

Based on cross-game analysis of 18 feudal games, the following priority ordering is recommended for gamedev-feudal development.

## Priority Tiers

### P0 — Core Must-Have
1. **[[Vassal loyalty systems|Vassal loyalty/rebellion system]]** — Core feudal tension; generates emergent narrative
2. **Land/[[demesne management|demesne management]]** — Land = power is the fundamental feudal principle

### P1 — High Priority
3. **Succession & inheritance** — Creates natural story arcs across generations
4. **Economy (simplified, meaningful chains)** — Must matter at all stages, not just early game

### P2 — Medium Priority
5. **Court and council system** — Where the feudal drama plays out
6. **[[Faction system]]** — Groups vassals around shared interests

### P3 — Lower Priority (Implement Later)
7. **Military/defense** — Important but secondary to social dynamics
8. **Religion/church relations** — Adds depth but can be complex to implement

## Mechanics to Avoid or Simplify

- **Complex production chains** — Use Against the Storm's approach: short, meaningful chains
- **Micromanagement of individual buildings** — Manor Lords' organic placement works better
- **Spreadsheet-only army combat** — Name commanders at minimum
- **Endless resource accumulation** — Add meaningful sinks: prestige projects, castle upgrades, court events
- **Single-axis loyalty systems** — Use at least 2-3 factors: opinion, obligation, fear/respect

## Scope Warning

The #1 lesson from market research: **feudal games fail when they try to do everything**. [[CK3|CK3]] took decades. [[Bannerlord]] spent 10 years. [[Manor Lords]] is still early access.

**Recommended vertical slice**: One domain, 5-10 vassals, basic economy, one succession cycle, one or two major events. Get that working and fun before expanding.
