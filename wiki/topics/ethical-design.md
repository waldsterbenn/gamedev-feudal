# Ethical Design

## Overview

Ethical design is a core positioning principle for the Feudal game project, representing an explicit rejection of dark patterns and exploitative game design practices.

## Project Commitment

The project positions itself with an explicit commitment to **ethical design**: "Learn feudalism by playing as a feudal lord — no grind, no dark patterns, no pay-to-win."

## Key Principles

### No Grind
The core gameplay loop should avoid repetitive grinding for resources or experience. Research indicates that games praised for accessibility (Against the Storm, Manor Lords) succeeded partly by avoiding grind-heavy mechanics. The recommended **Observe → Decide → Watch → Adapt** loop generates variety through emergent events, not repetition.

### No Dark Patterns
Dark patterns are design choices that manipulate players into spending more time or money than they intend. These include:
- Artificial scarcity designed to drive monetization
- Opaque pricing or loot box mechanics
- Progress walls that require payment to overcome
- Exploitative use of FOMO (fear of missing out)

### No Pay-to-Win
The project should ensure that player skill and decision-making, not financial investment, determine success.

## Educational Alignment

The ethical design position aligns with the project's **educational intent** — teaching players about feudalism through systems rather than exploiting engagement for revenue. This fills a market gap identified in [[market analysis medieval games]] as "casual/educational feudal games" — an underserved niche.

## Market Rationale

Players increasingly respond positively to ethical design practices. Games that provide transparent, respectful experiences (Against the Storm's consistent updates, Manor Lords' community transparency) achieve the highest review scores and strongest community loyalty.
