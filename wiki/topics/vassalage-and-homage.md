# Vassalage and Homage

## Overview

**Vassalage** was the ritual and legal bond between a [[lord]] and [[vassal]], forming the core relational structure of [[feudalism]]. It was a reciprocal contract of mutual obligations that bound every level of the [[feudal hierarchy]] together.

## The Homage Ceremony

The formal establishment of a lord-vassal relationship involved:

1. **Homage**: The vassal knelt before the lord, placed hands in the lord's hands, and swore an oath
2. **Fealty**: The vassal's sworn loyalty to the lord — a sacred oath
3. **Investiture**: The formal act by which the lord granted the [[fief]] to the vassal, often symbolized by handing over a clod of earth or a banner

## What the Vassal Owed the Lord

- **Military service**: Typically 40 days per year of armed service
- **Counsel**: Attendance at the lord's court to advise and judge
- **Financial aids**: Payments on specific occasions (lord's ransom, knighting of eldest son, marriage of eldest daughter)
- **Relief**: Inheritance tax upon entering a fief
- **Wardship and marriage rights**: If the heir was a minor, the lord controlled the fief and influenced marriage arrangements

## What the Lord Owed the Vassal

- **Protection**: Military defense of the vassal's lands
- **Justice**: Fair adjudication of disputes in the lord's court
- **Maintenance**: Ensuring the vassal could sustain their station (the fief itself)

## Multiple Vassalage Problem

A major structural weakness of feudalism: a vassal could hold fiefs from multiple lords, creating conflicting loyalties. The **Duke of Normandy** was technically a vassal of the King of France while simultaneously being King of England — a paradox that fueled centuries of Anglo-French conflict.

**English solution**: The Salisbury Oath (1086) required all major landowners to swear allegiance directly to the [[king]], overriding sub-vassal loyalties.

## Game Design Application

For [[feudal game design]], vassal relationships are the primary source of emergent narrative. Games should model:
- Multi-axis loyalty (opinion, obligation satisfaction, fear/respect, shared interests)
- Vassals with personalities, ambitions, and relationships with each other
- Rebellion as dramatic story generation, not checkbox failure
- Faction formation around shared grievances

The best implementation is considered to be [[crusader kings iii]]'s multi-layered model combining base opinion, feudal contracts, dread, factions, and power dynamics.
