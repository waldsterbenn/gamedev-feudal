# Game Design Takeaways

## Overview

Seven key takeaways from comprehensive analysis of 18 medieval/feudal games, representing the most important lessons for gamedev-feudal design.

## Seven Key Takeaways

### 1. Narrative Emergence is the Primary Value Driver
Systems should generate stories automatically, not just process numbers. Players remember events and characters, not spreadsheets.

### 2. Character Matters More Than Abstraction
Named [[vassal|vassals]], soldiers, and characters are worth the development cost. Players form emotional bonds with named entities.

### 3. Failure Should Redirect, Not Punish
A lost battle or rebellious vassal should create a new chapter, not a game over. Players want new opportunities after setbacks.

### 4. The Scale Paradox
Players want both granular control (this farm feeds 12 people) and grand overview (my kingdom spans three provinces). Offer meaningful zooms/layers.

### 5. Resist Perfect Optimization
Feudalism was messy, contradictory, and human. Clean game design should embrace some of that messy humanity.

### 6. Investment Grows Through Time and Naming
The longer a character exists in the player's world, the more they care when something happens to them.

### 7. Economy Must Matter at All Stages
Scarcity in early game, meaningful investment choices in late game. The #1 shared failure across the genre is end-game economy saturation.

## What to Steal from Best-in-Class Games

| Game | What to Steal | What to Avoid |
|------|--------------|---------------|
| **[[Crusader Kings III]]** | Vassal personality system, faction dynamics, succession as gameplay | Overwhelming complexity, DLC-heavy model |
| **[[Against the Storm]]** | Roguelite structure for replayability, short meaningful runs | Abstracted characters (race-based, no names) |
| **[[Manor Lords]]** | Visual city growth, organic placement, community transparency | Minimal feudal relationship simulation |
| **[[Medieval Dynasty]]** | First-person immersion, dynasty mechanics, tactile life simulation | Repetitive survival grind |
| **[[Pentiment]]** | Class dynamics, narrative depth, authentic social depiction | No gameplay simulation (pure narrative) |
| **[[Bannerlord]]** | Fiefdom management, companion system, tactical combat | Single-axis relationship system |
