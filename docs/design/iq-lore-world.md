# Interview Questionnaire: Lore & Game World

> **Purpose:** Extract creative direction, establish consistent world rules, and define the narrative framework that shapes all other game systems.
> **Format:** Open discussion — record answers in this document or as follow-up notes.
> **Duration:** ~60-90 minutes

---

## Section 1: Core World Identity

*Goal: Establish the foundational feel and identity of the game world.*

1. **Describe the game world in three words.**
   - Why these three words? What should the player feel the moment they see the world?

2. **What is the single most important historical event that shaped this world?**
   - How does it still affect the player today (ruins, laws, fear, culture)?

3. **Who holds power in this world?**
   - Is it a single ruler, a council, guilds, the church, military, or something else?
   - How does the player perceive and interact with this power structure?

4. **What is one thing about this world that is unexpected or subverted compared to real medieval/feudal history?**
   - What makes this world *yours* rather than a textbook re-skin?

---

## Section 2: Geography & Space

*Goal: Define the physical spaces the player will traverse.*

5. **Think about how the world is organized geographically. Is it one large connected map, separate regions, or instanced locations?**
   - What is your gut instinct, and why?

6. **Name three distinct biomes or regions in the world.**
   - What makes each unique visually, culturally, and mechanically?
   - Example: Dense forest with rebel camps vs. open farmland under tight lord control.

7. **How does the environment itself tell a story?**
   - Environmental storytelling examples: destroyed bridges, abandoned watchtowers, pilgrimage routes, overgrown battlefields.

8. **What is the scale of the world?**
   - Walking distance between major locations? Hours of gameplay per region?

---

## Section 3: Factions & Politics

*Goal: Define who the player interacts with and how allegiances work.*

9. **List the major factions or power groups.**
   - What does each want? What does each fear?
   - How do they relate to each other (enemies, uneasy allies, competing)?

10. **Where does the player fit into the feudal hierarchy?**
   - Are they a lord, a rising knight, a displaced noble, a peasant revolutionary?
   - Can the player's position change through gameplay?

11. **How will players navigate faction allegiance?**
   - Locked to one faction, reputation-based, or free to switch?
   - What are the consequences of betrayal or loyalty?

12. **What role do the common people play?**
   - Are they background flavor, a resource to manage, or a force that can rebel?
   - How does the world react to the player's treatment of them?

---

## Section 4: Culture & Daily Life

*Goal: Ground the world in tangible, lived-in detail.*

13. **What are the major cultural touchstones of this world?**
   - Religion, festivals, martial traditions, legal customs, superstitions?

14. **What does a common person's daily life look like?**
   - What do they eat, fear, celebrate, complain about?
   - This informs NPC behavior, ambient dialogue, and world activity.

15. **What conflicts or tensions are bubbling under the surface?**
   - Class struggle, succession crisis, foreign invasion, plague, heresy?
   - How can the player interact with or resolve these?

---

## Section 5: Lore Integration with Gameplay

*Goal: Ensure lore serves gameplay, not the other way around.*

16. **How will lore be delivered to the player?**
   - Environmental storytelling, NPC dialogue, codex entries, quests, item descriptions, or a mix?
   - What is the ratio of show vs. tell?

17. **Are there secrets or hidden lore that reward exploration?**
   - What's an example of a lore secret you'd want players to discover on their own?

18. **What is the tone of the narrative?**
   - Gritty and realistic, heroic fantasy, dark and cynical, lighthearted, morally gray?
   - What games or media capture a similar tone?

19. **How deep does the lore need to be at launch vs. post-launch?**
   - What is the minimum viable lore for a compelling vertical slice?
   - What can be expanded later?

## Facilitator Notes

- **Let ideas flow first, filter later.** Don't shut down wild ideas in the brainstorm. Separate creation from evaluation.
- **Anchor in player experience.** Every lore decision should answer: "How does the player encounter this?"
- **Consistency over complexity.** A simple, coherent world beats a sprawling, contradictory one.
- **Leave gaps.** Don't define everything. Unexplained areas let level design and writing fill space organically.
- **Check against pillars.** If a lore element doesn't support a game design pillar, cut or reframe it.
